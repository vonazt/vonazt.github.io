About Me

All my life I’ve been obsessed with culture, creativity and problem-solving, whether it’s reading every kind of book I can find, playing 2D platformers, engaging in political debate, finding the perfect chilli recipe, travelling to the far corners of the world, building an eclectic record collection, solving crosswords, doing pub quizzes or supporting Saracens rugby. I have a voracious appetite for whatever is happening in the world.

Initially after graduating I worked in journalism to support myself while writing a book. However, while I found the journalism industry interesting, the editorial work itself was neither as challenging nor as rewarding as I’d hoped, and writing was unsustainable by itself.

While I was looking around for other careers, I tried a bit of coding and immediately loved it. Moving into development as a career was an easy choice for me; it ticked all the boxes in both a personal and professional sense. On the WDI course at General Assembly I feel like I’ve thrived - I’ve been constantly challenged in terms of having to learn new things, while also exercising all my creative and problem-solving skills to produce work that I’m proud of. I feel a constant sense of energy and commitment to learning that I’m excited to take into my future career.

Ideally, I’d like to be part of a small, dynamic, socially conscious team working on a variety of hard problems that need solving - I like to be constantly taking on new challenges and learning new skills, which is another reason why I was initially attracted to the ever-evolving world of tech. My main priorities would be learning as much as possible from the people around me, while also doing work that I care about.
